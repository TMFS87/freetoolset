❓ FAQ

What are well-known ports?

Well-known ports range from 0 to 1023 and are assigned by IANA (Internet Assigned Numbers Authority) to specific services and protocols. Examples include port 80 for HTTP, 443 for HTTPS, and 22 for SSH.

What is the difference between TCP and UDP?

TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) is connection-oriented, ensuring reliable data delivery with error checking. UDP (User Datagram Protocol) is connectionless and faster but does not guarantee delivery. Some services use both protocols on the same port number.

What does "high risk" mean?

High-risk ports are commonly targeted by attackers due to known vulnerabilities, weak authentication, or the sensitive nature of the data they handle. Examples include Telnet (23), FTP (21), and RDP (3389). These ports should be firewalled or secured with encryption where possible.

Why are some ports marked as TLS?

TLS (Transport Layer Security) ports use encryption to protect data in transit. For example, HTTPS (443), IMAPS (993), and SMTPS (465) are TLS-secured versions of their plaintext counterparts. Using TLS-encrypted ports is strongly recommended.

How many ports are there in total?

There are 65,535 TCP ports and 65,535 UDP ports. Ports 0–1023 are well-known ports, 1024–49151 are registered ports, and 49152–65535 are dynamic/private ports. This tool covers the most commonly used ports.
